# The German Century: 1860–1913

**Live site:** [jzrucker.github.io/german-century](https://jzrucker.github.io/german-century/)

---

## The honest reason this exists

I listen to somewhere between 1,000 and 2,000 hours of history podcasts a year. Always on — at the gym, at work, walking around. I've listened to everything worth listening to in the English-language history podcast space. Fall of Civilizations. Revolutions. Both Sides of the Wire. The Napoleon podcast. All of it.

The era I couldn't find — anywhere, in any format — was Imperial Germany. 1860 to 1913. The most dynamic civilization on earth in that period, and almost nobody in English has touched it seriously. So I built it myself.

I go to the gym every day. I needed something to listen to. This is that thing.

---

## What it actually is

An AI-generated history podcast narrated by a fictional character — Prof. Josef Ezekiel Von Gieslingen, a retired German academic who taught at Rutgers and Penn State, whose great-grandfather heard the cannons at Königgrätz, and who refuses to call this a podcast.

The scripts are researched and written with Claude. The voice is generated with ElevenLabs, cloned from a real German historian speaking English. The website looks like it was built by someone's grandson in 2003, because that is the point.

---

## The deeper idea

Beyond keeping me entertained at the gym — this project is about something I find genuinely interesting: whether you can create a believable persistent AI persona. Not a chatbot. Not a faceless narrator. A real character with a history, a voice, recurring obsessions, a specific point of view, and content people actually want to spend time with.

Josef is one instance of that idea. The methodology that produces Josef — character design, voice cloning, long-form script generation, persona-driven content — is something else entirely. That's the startup-adjacent thought underneath the history podcast.
